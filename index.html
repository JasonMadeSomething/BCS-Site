Frequently Asked Questions

What if I am the only person in my business?

If you are the only owner, member, partner, or person in your business enter your name in sections 1 â€“ 4 and provide your meeting notes on the back of our order form or attach additional pages. You can be the only person in your business and still do annual minutes. These annual minutes are good for record keeping and are in full compliance with the law.

Why did I receive an order form?

All business and corporations that are (active and operating) within the state are supposed to complete annual minutes that include certain information pertaining to the businesses activities. These minutes also include a list of corporate officers, directors and shareholders if there are any. We prepare these minutes with the information you provide for a fee and send them back to you for your records.

Am I required to fill out this form?

While corporations are required to keep annual minutes and every business should for good records, it is not mandatory that you use our service for your annual minute needs. Annual minutes may also be prepared by corporate officers, some accountants, and attorneys.

Is this different than an annual business report filing fee?

Yes. Annual minutes do not satisfy your annual filing fee required by the state to maintain your ACTIVE status and should not be confused with the state's annual filing fee. These are two completely different things.

What if I already paid or have done my minutes?

If you have already paid for or have your annual minutes, there is no need to complete our order form unless you would like to have us prepare them for you going forward or have multiple meetings throughout the course of the year that you would like to have prepared through our services.

How do I stop receiving order forms?

If you do not wish to receive future mailings from our company, please use the contact form and we will do the rest.
